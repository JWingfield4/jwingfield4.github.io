DEFENDING THE GULF

The Gulf Coast has some of the most unique fortifications seen in America

Many of these old port and coastal forts were built by the Spanish and French Empire's in the 17th and 18th centuries.

One interesting example is Fort San Carlos de Barrancas, later known just as Fort Barrancas. Fort Barrancas was originally built in 1698 by the Spanish Empire. It was partially destroyed by Natives and French in 1707 and 1719. After the seven years war, the land was sceeded to the British who used the fort as a harbor fortification. After the American Revolution, the Spanish reclaimed the fort in 1781 and rebuilt the fortification in 1797. Over the years, the fort had gone between the US, Britian, the Confederates, and back to the US.

The Fort was finally decomissioned in 1947, two years after WW2. It is the only Fort in the United States to have been used by 5 different nations.
